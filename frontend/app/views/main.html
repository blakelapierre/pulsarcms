Occupy the Data Center

Pulsar is an entirely new direction in content management systems. It is also an entirely new direction in social networking. It also offers the most advanced presentation system and content creation tools of any CMS available. Pulsar can be hosted on modest hardware, is very easy to set up, use and maintain and is just getting started.

It's all being created and freely given away to prove whether the MEAN stack is ready for large-scale work. And, that's being done so I can finally zip the lips of "IT Professionals" who have no idea what they are talking about and have found themselves on my bad side.

Pulsar is similar to blogging software. But, instead of posts, Pulsar creates pulses. Pulses range from static documents similar to standard blog posts created in Pulsar Composer to highly interactive, dynamic video games and other applications created in Pulsar Studio. Watch Moving Parts, an work-in-progress example of what Pulsar Studio can already do. Then, have a look at Prototype, a finished example of what Pulsar Composer can do.

Pulsar is so open and modular that you can build your own applications against the Pulsar API server using nothing more than your web browser, and actually have every feature of Pulsar integrated into whatever you're building (a Composer document or a Studio application).

From personal blogs to news sites to music sites to online games to enterprise applications of all types, Pulsar wants to be a technology you can use to quickly meet your content and website management goals. It is easy to deploy, easy to maintain, handles remarkable levels of traffic and scale and sits atop some of the best modern technology.
