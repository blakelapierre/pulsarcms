What's the Coolest Thing Pulsar Currently Does?

The following is an example of Pulsar's built-in SoundCloud player widget. This is not the SoundCloud widget - it's custom Pulsar code.

Using Pulsar Composer's in-place editor, you just put the cursor where you want the sound, click the SoundCloud button in the toolbar, paste the URL and publish the pulse. This Pulsar SoundCloud Player is the result:

This widget requires Google Chrome for the full/best experience. Your mileage may vary in different browsers, mobile, etc. This is a demonstration of just how far Pulsar can push a browser today. Last I checked, yes, it works on Android in Chrome including the WebGL fullscreen visualizer if your hardware supports it.

What Are You Seeing?

That's not a screenshot. Press play and go fullscreen.

That is an HTML5 presentation styled with CSS3. No third-party plugins are in use. That is not the SoundCloud "widget", but it does use the SoundCloud API to stream audio directly into the Web Audio API. From there, the audio is decomposed into separate left/right streams and analyzed for a variety of information. Bass is separated into "red", midrange frequencies are separated into "green" and high-frequency audio is separated out into "blue". A 2D <canvas> element is used to render the audio frequency spectrum analyzer and time domain waveform analyzer.

The "code" to embed the Pulsar SoundCloud audio player into a pulse is: 

<pulsar:soundcloud-player
  data-sound-url="https://soundcloud.com/edm/sick-wid-it-by-virus-syndicate"
  ng-controller="PulsarSoundCloudPlayerCtrl"
  ></pulsar:soundcloud-player>

The fullscreen player (hit the fullscreen button) additionally uses WebGL to implement a full 3D music visualizer. The primary audio frequency powers (bass, mids and treble) are being used to modulate a variety of parameters in the control logic of the 3D scene. The size of the Starlight is controlled along X for bass, Y for mids and Z for treble (colored red, green and blue, respectively). The Starlight also emits light with an RGB mix that matches the audio frequency powers.

The Starlight visualizer runs at 60fps on modest hardware without using a single plugin. It's just the browser. The browser is only running Pulsar. Pulsar is using Web Audio, WebGL/Three.js, <canvas> and a ton of JavaScript. End of story.
