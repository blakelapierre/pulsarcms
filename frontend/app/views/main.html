What Is This?

You are on a web site operated by Rob Colbert, a veteran full-stack to low-level network and "Web" software developer. It is running his personal software project: Pulsar. The YouTube video above shows Pulsar doing what it does. If you have Google Chrome, find it under Demos -> Moving Parts.

The public is welcomed to participate on a variety of levels. The fastest and most helpful way is to create a test account and post content here. As you do, feel free to share any feedback you might have. Another way to get involved is to install Pulsar and run your own instance. A valid use of Pulsar would be as a family's personal server to share photos with friends and family without the advertising and privacy violations and incessant game invites of Facebook.

Pulsar is similar to blogging software. But, instead of posts, Pulsar creates pulses. Pulses range from static documents similar to standard blog posts created in Pulsar Composer to highly interactive, dynamic video games and other applications created in Pulsar Studio. Watch Moving Parts, a work-in-progress example of what Pulsar Studio can already do. Next, have a look at Prototype, a finished example of what Pulsar Studio can do.

Pulsar is distributed under the MIT open source license. You are entirely free to have and use Pulsar in whatever way you see fit. Build your own applications that run directly in Pulsar against using nothing more than your web browser and Pulsar Composer or Pulsar Studio.

From personal blogs to news sites to music sites to online games to web applications of all types, Pulsar wants to be a technology you can use to quickly meet your content creation and website management goals. It is easy to deploy, very easy to maintain, handles remarkable levels of traffic with no single points of failure end-to-end and sits atop some of the best modern technology that money can't buy.

Pulsar even has a Dislike button. You just can't see it, yet, because I'm still building the earliest of features in the UI. If it's too early, I understand. But, stop back each week. You'll see :)
