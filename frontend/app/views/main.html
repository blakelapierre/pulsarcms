When?

Pulsar is an Agile software product with continuous integration to production. That pretty much means that, from this moment forward, Pulsar just keeps becoming more of what is wanted, less of what isn't wanted and you get the updates the very moment they pass tests and, when possible, peer review.

It's usable right now, it'll never (ever) be done and don't expect a Beta label. Those are outdated.

Who?

Families who want to own their data and the network that carries it. Small, medium and large businesses that want a manageable web site management system. Intercontinental enterprises that want something that integrates well into their existing front end. A lot of people will simply use the free online service and not host anything. But, they'll all succeed at publishing content online in a way that works for them.

Why?

You failed to explain the value of Facebook owning your data, and would rather personally own that data. You also don't want or need to build the whole system yourself. We do.

Also, there is a desire to prove that MongoDB and Node.js are, "Ready for production." This buzzphrase is standing between people and their continued good health. So, Pulsar partly exists to shove this philosophical discussion out of the way of progress.

Near Future

Constellation

Once Pulsar is more complete, Constellation will be launched. The Constellation service wants to become the planet's first decentralized social network. It is intended to be used by anyone who wants to own their data and the conversation around it instead of relying on monolithic companies like Facebook, Twitter or anyone but themselves.

Long-Term

As the planet's first truly distributed, decentralized social network enabling people to communicate in fun new ways while owning their data, Pulsar and Constellation may or may not change all of our lives, but it might touch a few. And, that's the hope. That's all there is to it. Touch a few lives and help them make positive changes. Period. Anything else that happens along the way will be considered an unitended but necessary step along the path.
