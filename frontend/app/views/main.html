Pulsar Helps People Create and Communicate

Do you feel limited by social media? I do. For example, what are your thoughts about:

  • 140-character messages (in 2014)
  • Selectively enforced authoritarian content restriction policies prohibiting whatever you wanted or needed to post
  • Insane violations of your right to privacy and other rights
  • Inadequate content creation tools

Most social networks don't allow for text formatting like bold or italics to help you get your point across. The ones that do don't offer any control over how the media you share like photos and/or videos will appear within a post. You simply get to "attach" them.

I know I can certainly think of better ways to share a weddig video than, for example, "Here, let me 'attach' this video and describe my unbounded joy with the 80 characters left for my use after that YouTube URL is in the post. And, I'd say you get what you pay for, but Pulsar is also entirely free.

These concerns and especially the cavalier attitude shown toward privacy by today's modern social networks have all added up to a lot of end user frustration. People don't trust these services like they once did.

Privacy is no longer a cavalier afterthought. End User Licence Agreements (EULAs) are actually being read. It's all becoming a horrible end user experience, and that's simply because no one can understand it all anymore. It's gotten frustrating and completely out of hand for a lot of people.

Pulsar has two jobs: Help people create; and help people share what they've created. It does this effectively while staying entirely out of their way. The basic philosophy is for Pulsar to be helpful while not being irritating because I genuinely fucking despise irritating bullshit and people. I only guarantee Pulsar won't be bullshit. I can't guarantee what the people using it will be like. Rob Colbert (Pulsar's creator and lead programmer)

Pulsar is worth exploring. It's an easier and more technologically advanced way to manage content online, keep in touch with and share things those who matter most to you and even develop a whole new application that does whatever you need it to do. Your a-ha moment will be the realization that you own your data. Because you control your data on Pulsar. And, if you run your own Pulsar instance, its data is entirely yours.

What's the Coolest Thing Pulsar Currently Does?

The following is an example of Pulsar's built-in SoundCloud player widget. This is not the SoundCloud widget - it's custom Pulsar code.

Using Pulsar Composer's in-place editor, you just put the cursor where you want the sound, click the SoundCloud button in the toolbar, paste the URL and publish the pulse. This Pulsar SoundCloud Player is the result:

This widget requires Google Chrome for the full/best experience. Your mileage may vary in different browsers, mobile, etc. This is a demonstration of just how far Pulsar can push a browser today. Last I checked, yes, it works on Android in Chrome including the WebGL fullscreen visualizer if your hardware supports it.

What Are You Seeing?

That's not a screenshot. Press play and go fullscreen.

That is an HTML5 presentation styled with CSS3. No third-party plugins are in use. That is not the SoundCloud "widget", but it does use the SoundCloud API to stream audio directly into the Web Audio API. From there, the audio is decomposed into separate left/right streams and analyzed for a variety of information. Bass is separated into "red", midrange frequencies are separated into "green" and high-frequency audio is separated out into "blue". A 2D <canvas> element is used to render the audio frequency spectrum analyzer and time domain waveform analyzer.

The "code" to embed the Pulsar SoundCloud audio player into a pulse is: 

<pulsar:soundcloud-player
  data-sound-url="https://soundcloud.com/edm/sick-wid-it-by-virus-syndicate"
  ng-controller="PulsarSoundCloudPlayerCtrl"
  ></pulsar:soundcloud-player>

The fullscreen player (hit the fullscreen button) additionally uses WebGL to implement a full 3D music visualizer. The primary audio frequency powers (bass, mids and treble) are being used to modulate a variety of parameters in the control logic of the 3D scene. The size of the Starlight is controlled along X for bass, Y for mids and Z for treble (colored red, green and blue, respectively). The Starlight also emits light with an RGB mix that matches the audio frequency powers.

The Starlight visualizer runs at 60fps on modest hardware without using a single plugin. It's just the browser. The browser is only running Pulsar. Pulsar is using Web Audio, WebGL/Three.js, <canvas> and a ton of JavaScript. End of story.

Who Should Use Pulsar?

On some online/social networks, it can genuinely feel like you're being reprimanded by the high school principal for talking without raising your hand, being late or for having to use the bathroom without a hall pass. That's not freedom.

Modern bloggers, music producers, film and video producers, talk show hosts, DJs and MCs and pretty much anyone out there with something to say or do professionally or as a hobby should already be looking at Pulsar. This is your chance to shape an emerging new technology. It's happening right now.

So, if you are or know a creative person who struggles with the general frustrations of online services, please send them my way. I'd love to talk about how Pulsar can specifically help them.

I know. Yet another account? Yet another password? Yet another identity to manage online? Why can't it just take my Facebook, Twitter or Google credentials? It can. That's up to you. When you run your own Pulsar, you can enable those features.

My personal Pulsar's goal, while helping people create and communicate, is to allow them the exciting freedom that comes along with actually owning your own data. To meet that goal, Pulsar does not communicate with 3rd-party authentication service providers. It doesn't want them to know when you're using it.
