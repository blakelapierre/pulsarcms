Is This a Beta?

Nope. Pulsar is an Agile software product with continuous integration to production. That pretty much means that, from this moment forward, Pulsar just keeps becoming more of what is wanted, less of what isn't wanted and you get the updates the very moment they pass tests and, when possible, peer review.

It's usable right now, it'll never (ever) be done and don't expect a Beta label. Those are outdated.

Who Uses Pulsar?

Families who want to own their data and the network that privately carries it. Small, medium and large businesses that want a manageable web site management system. Intercontinental enterprises that want usable technology that integrates well into their existing front end. Many will use the free online service and not host anything. Some will subscribe to the online service for enhanced features and a more professional/custom online appearance. They'll all succeed at publishing content online in a way that works for them.

Why Use Pulsar?

You failed to explain the value of Facebook owning your data, and would rather personally own that data. You also don't want or need to build the whole system yourself. We do.

Also, there is a desire to prove that MongoDB and Node.js are, "Ready for production." This buzzphrase is standing between people and their continued good health. So, Pulsar partly exists to shove this philosophical discussion out of the way of progress.

Project Goals

The idea to create Pulsar happened Sunday, Feb 9, 2014, after I'd finally had enough of arguing for the use of Node.js and MongoDB using only words. I am sick of trying to convince people that these tools are all of enterprise grade and production ready. So, here it all is. I'm doing enterprise development on the MEAN stack, and it's running in the cloud on production servers separately from my development network at home.

Pulsar was created with the following primary goals:

Be Easy
for content creators
for consumers/users
for IT professionals and non-IT professionals
Be Prolific
Pulsar must provide the most advanced content publishing capabilities to the largest number of people. Constellation must connect people who want to share with one another.
Be Enterprise-Grade
Acknowledge that, initially, commodity hosting is a challenge
Solve commodity hosting shortages with public service for all
Offer a repeatable deployment toolset for self-hosted users
Support integration with custom deployment toolchains for enterprise users
Have a documented, repeatable development environment

The end goal is to enable people to share content online while owning the content they share. A Pulsar instance will be no more public than its owner configures it to be. No Pulsar is mandated to participate in Constellation and can always exist as its own island powering its own private and highly secure community.

Adoption Goals

Pulsar is currently in an early-adopter phase of rapid-fire development. It is not advised to deploy Pulsar for production service at this time. The target audience looks like:

  • Technophiles and "gadget geeks" who want to experiment with all emerging technology.
  • Software developers who want to create custom Pulsar clients, mobile apps and other solutions using the Pulsar content platform.
  • API developers who want to easily and quickly create custom Node.js powered applications. MongoDB is not a requirement for anything built in addition to Pulsar's core services. MongoDB is absolutely required for Pulsar's core. This reqirement will change if and only if MongoDB no longer exists.
  • Enterprise software developers looking for a case study being conducted in public to determine whether enterprise-grade software development is possible on the MEAN stack and whether the MEAN stack is, "Production ready."
  • Content creators curious about getting to experiment with a whole new publishing paradigm. View the current prototype to see an example of what Pulsar can do :)

Pulsar's next target audience will be progressive advertising agencies with advanced messages to deliver, educational institutions with important communcations to enable and security experts to tear it to shreds and make sure it stays on track with industry best practices and standards (or ahead of them, if posible).

Coming Soon: Constellation

Once Pulsar is more complete, Constellation will be launched. The Constellation service wants to become the planet's first decentralized social network. It is intended to be used by anyone who wants to own their data and the conversation around it instead of relying on monolithic companies like Facebook, Twitter or anyone but themselves.

As the planet's first truly distributed, decentralized social network enabling people to communicate in fun new ways while owning their data, Pulsar and Constellation may or may not change all of our lives, but it might touch a few. And, that's the hope. That's all there is to it. Touch a few lives and help them make positive changes.
