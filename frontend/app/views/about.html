Who Built Pulsar?

Rob Colbert
creator
<rob.isConnected@gmail.com>
http://www.linkedin.com/in/robcolbert

A computer programmer (Rob) snapped the fuck out one day. After two straight decades in computer software, Rob quit the last programming job he'll ever work on purpose. Because, after more than a decade of fighting with various IT assholes over this & that at various companies, Rob finally asked, "Why is this always a fight? Am I perhaps the one who is retarded?" And, with that in mind, Rob set off on a journey to find out. Pulsar is that journey.

Because, as far as Rob could see from his desk in application developer land, it was no longer worth trying to succeed. Here he is bending over backwards to keep himself current for all these years while raising a family, burying a son, always working overtime... and... for what? A never-ending fight with a bunch of retarded assholes? Yes, Rob fucking hates you. ALL of you.

So, he gave up the whole concept of working in corporate environments with other people for money. He also never wants to need to work with other human beings again (in software). (Ever). He simply can't trust that process anymore to ensure his and his family's needs are met. It does not work. He can not and will not succeed in your fucked-up, idiotic, nepotistic, corrupt, backward and apathetic world. He likes to feel something for what he does. So, he left that stagnant, stress-filled existence behind in a place where he really does not want to be anymore (Pittsburgh, PA).

And, until he figures out what he's going to actually be doing next and where that might be located, he has eschewed all manner of personal responsibility down to not even making his wife's car payments while not giving a single fuck. Nothing along that path worked. Not one single thing. At all. Rob doesn't care if he ends up divorced, homeless, penniless and dead. He does not and will not write software for money in corporate environments anymore. And, as a direct result, he outright owns anything he builds, now.
