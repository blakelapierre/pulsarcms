A computer programmer (Rob Colbert, me) snapped the fuck out one day. After two straight decades in computer software, I decided to shoot and bury my permanent, full-time corporate experience. Because, after more than a decade of fighting with various IT assholes over this & that at various companies, I finally asked, "Why is this always a fight? Am I perhaps the one who is flawed in my pursuit of progress?"

So, with this new mantra in mind, I set off on a journey to find out. Pulsar is that journey. That's all it is. I have questions to answer for myself while giving no fucks.

Because, as far as I could see from my desk in application developer land, it was no longer worth trying to succeed. There I was...bending over backwards to keep myself current for all these years...while raising a family, burying a son, working way too God-damned much overtime due to people's horrible decisions and even worse technology... and... for what? A never-ending fight with a bunch of assholes when it comes time to make improvements?

Fuuuuuuuuuuuck you. I fucking hate you and the experiences you put me through. And, I am going to keep shredding and burning this career until I find my way down, up or sideways to a place where workable technology can be used to solve interesting or fun problems while creating compelling online experiences. That's all I want to do.

Until then, I can hunt. I'm not gonna starve. So, yeah...if you're one of the fuckers who's stood in my way along my past paths: Fuck you. If you hate me, it's only because I fucking left you in yesterday where you chose to stay. YOU chose that, not me. So...

Best regards,
-Rob
