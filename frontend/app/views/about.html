A computer programmer (Rob, me) snapped the fuck out one day. After two straight decades in computer software, I decided to strangle and bury my permanent, full-time corporate experience. Because, after more than a decade of fighting with various IT assholes over this & that at various companies, I finally asked, "Why is this always a fight? Am I perhaps the one who is flawed in my pursuit of progress?" And, with that in mind, I set off on a journey to fucking find out. And, Pulsar is that journey. That's all it is. I have questions to answer for myself while giving no fucks.

Because, as far as I could see from my desk in application developer land, it was no longer worth trying to succeed. Here I am bending over backwards to keep myself current for all these years while raising a family, burying a son, working way too God-damned much overtime due to bad decisions and worse technology... and... for what? A never-ending fight with a bunch of assholes? Yes, motherfuckers: I fucking hate you and the experiences you share.

So, until I figure out what the fuck I'm going to actually be doing next and where that might be located or involve, I'm not interested in whatever you're saying. And, that's because I'm too fucking busy right here making all this progress while trying to independently pay my rent and bills, motherfucker.

I can hunt. I'm not gonna starve. And, I am fucking doing it this way because motherfuck an investor. If I'm right, I'll fucking own this. You had your fucking chances. I've gone a very nonviolent form of postal. Fuck you and everything you are.

Best regards,
-Rob
