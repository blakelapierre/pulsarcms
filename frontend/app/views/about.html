A computer programmer (Rob) snapped the fuck out one day. After two straight decades in computer software, Rob quit the last perm/full-time programming job he'll ever work. Because, after more than a decade of fighting with various IT assholes over this & that at various companies, Rob finally asked, "Why is this always a fight? Am I perhaps the one who is flawed in my pursuit of progress?" And, with that in mind, Rob set off on a journey to find out. Pulsar is that journey.

Because, as far as Rob could see from his desk in application developer land, it was no longer worth trying to succeed. Here he is bending over backwards to keep himself current for all these years while raising a family, burying a son, always working overtime... and... for what? A never-ending fight with a bunch of assholes? Yes, I fucking hate you.

And, until he figures out what he's going to actually be doing next and where that might be located, he's not interested in whatever you're saying because he's too fucking busy right here making all this progress while trying to independently pay the rent and bills. Because fuck an investor, etc., I intend to own this.
