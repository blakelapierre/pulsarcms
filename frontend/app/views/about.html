I've Had Enough

You ever feel like you're sitting in a meeting being asked to do something in a way for which you know a simpler, more feasible way exists? Imagine, for example, being constantly forced (as a carpenter) to use a jackhammer when the task is roofing shingles. Can it be done? Sure. With enough effort. But, a simpler approach does exist. The carpenter may prefer to use a simple hammer. It's a job for which the jackhammer does not make things go faster or better. Nail gun? Yes. Jackhammer? Too much.

I'm also talking about the kind of feeling that tends to set in after hearing requests to use WordPress, Liferay or perhaps Drupal to build a highly interactive non-blog site that works nothing like they do. It's the feeling that happens when being asked to use J2EE spanning all of Spring, Struts, Hibernate, portal containers and a spendy Oracle database to build the simplest of CRUD apps like an in-house PTO request form that sees 12 "hits" per month.

I get that sinking feeling all the time. In fact, in a former life, I got this feeling almost daily. And, yes...beyond a point...I decided I'd simply had enough.

So, Who Are You?

My name is Rob Colbert.

I am a veteran computer programmer with over 20 years of experience in video games, low-level networking, systems and embedded software development. I've designed, written, implemented, tested, deployed and/or maintained systems in the domains of single-player and online games, backbone-grade network equipment, enterprise applications, educational software and training simulations. I've hand-built 2D and 3D graphics and presentation systems, audio processing and streaming libraries, entire online gaming systems and the tools/asset pipelines to support them all.

I've done these things in contributing, lead and architect roles since about 1994. I have been constantly learning to program computers since about 1987. And, I've always loved what I've gotten to do: Create. It's why I write code. The past ten years of my career, however, has been spent fighting non-stop with various "IT Professionals" over this & that at various companies in my past wake. And, I eventually realized that I'm simply too advanced for the average IT Professional's comfort level.

The Expert

Would you believe I've been asked to create seven red perpendicular lines using green and transparent ink in the form of a kitten more than once in my career? The kitten baloon only happened once, though.

So, I've deicded to stop trying to succeed along that whole path. They can keep it. Instead, I've changed the questions I am asking. I've stopped asking, "How do I show them a better way?" or, "How do I stay sane here?" I finally realized that I don't have to care. So, I've simply stopped asking those goofy questions. And, today? I guess I'm only interested in knowing:

Why is this always a fight? Am I perhaps the one who is flawed in my pursuit of innovation?

With this new mantra in mind, I set off on a journey to find the answer. Pulsar is that journey. And, it's a personal journey in open source community membership, modern technology stacks and patience. Because today, the page you're reading is being served by Pulsar, an application of my own creation sitting atop the MEAN stack. And, it's running in production without issues. Ever.

Pieces of the MEAN stack such as MongoDB may not be the perfect tool for every job. True statement. But, I argue it is perfectly acceptable for use when I say it is. And, I argue that on the basis that the 20 years of experience known as, "Rob Colbert," has been doing the sort of shit you go home and dream and/or have nightmares about for all twenty of these years.

I'm simply sick of having some "IT Professional" constantly trying to tell me how to do my job just because the words coming out of my mouth aren't: Oracle, MySQL and/or Java.
